If you get a puncture, take off the wheel (if it’s a rear drop it down into the outside sprocket first, as this will help you know where to hang the chain when putting the wheel back on). Use your tyre levers to remove one side of the tyre and then pull out the tube.

It is always easy to spot an inexperienced cyclist. They are trying to churn a huge gear with their shoulders. You will see their shoulders move at least 12-18 inches with each pedal stroke. Bikes nowadays can have anything up to 33 gears. Even if you only have 10, use them to keep a nice steady economical cadence from the energy point of view.
